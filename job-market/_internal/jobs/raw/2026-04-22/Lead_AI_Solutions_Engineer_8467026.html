Job Description:

DataRobot delivers AI that maximizes impact and minimizes business risk. Our platform and applications integrate into core business processes so teams can develop, deliver, and govern AI at scale. DataRobot empowers practitioners to deliver predictive and generative AI, and enables leaders to secure their AI assets. Organizations worldwide rely on DataRobot for AI that makes sense for their business — today and in the future. 

The Principal AI Solutions Engineer plays a pivotal role in helping enterprise customers see, feel, and recognize the value of agentic AI, fast. This role sits at the intersection of business understanding, AI application design, and polished product execution. The primary mission is to rapidly design and build enterprise-grade MVP (Minimum Viable Product) agentic AI applications, often within days—that clearly demonstrate how AI agents can transform real business processes.

You will partner closely with prospective customers, subject matter experts, and pre-sales team members to turn ambiguous business problems into compelling, production‑inspired prototypes. These MVPs are not throwaway demos; they are credible, extensible foundations that showcase technical depth, strong UX/UI taste, and modern AI architecture, while remaining pragmatic and fast to deliver.

Required Skills, Expertise & Experience

This role requires senior-level judgment: deep hands-on building experience, strong customer-facing intuition, and the ability to make pragmatic tradeoffs under real-world constraints.

Core Technical Skills

  • Strong hands-on experience building agentic AI applications using modern LLM frameworks and orchestration patterns.

  • Solid backend engineering skills (e.g., Python, Agent Skills, MCP, OpenAI Agents SDK, Claude Code, Cursor).

  • Experience designing and running Agent Skills, MCP servers, tool APIs, and external system integrations.

  • Familiarity with agent tracing, observability, logging, and evaluation techniques for AI applications.

Frontend & Design Taste

  • Proficiency with React and modern frontend tooling.

  • Experience using shadcn/ui or similar design systems to create clean, enterprise-ready UIs.

  • Strong UX instincts—able to simplify complex workflows and present AI behavior transparently and confidently.

Problem Solving & Communication

  • Exceptional ability to move from ambiguity to clarity quickly.

  • Strong verbal and written communication skills; comfortable leading technical conversations with non‑technical stakeholders.

  • Ability to explain complex AI concepts in a grounded, value‑driven, and honest way.

Education & Experience

  • 10+ years of experience in software engineering, applied AI, hands-on technical consulting, or technical product roles.

  • Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Engineering, or a related field—or equivalent practical experience.

  • Advanced degree or research background in AI/ML is a plus, but not required.

  • Ability to travel up to 25% in average to customer meetings, workshops, industry conferences, and events.

What you need to know about the Los Angeles Tech Scene

Los Angeles is a global leader in entertainment, so it’s no surprise that many of the biggest players in streaming, digital media and game development call the city home. But the city boasts plenty of non-entertainment innovation as well, with tech companies spanning verticals like AI, fintech, e-commerce and biotech. With major universities like Caltech, UCLA, USC and the nearby UC Irvine, the city has a steady supply of top-flight tech and engineering talent — not counting the graduates flocking to Los Angeles from across the world to enjoy its beaches, culture and year-round temperate climate.

Key Facts About Los Angeles Tech

  • Number of Tech Workers: 375,800; 5.5% of overall workforce (2024 CompTIA survey)
  • Major Tech Employers: Snap, Netflix, SpaceX, Disney, Google
  • Key Industries: Artificial intelligence, adtech, media, software, game development
  • Funding Landscape: $11.6 billion in venture capital funding in 2024 (Pitchbook)
  • Notable Investors: Strong Ventures, Fifth Wall, Upfront Ventures, Mucker Capital, Kittyhawk Ventures
  • Research Centers and Universities: California Institute of Technology, UCLA, University of Southern California, UC Irvine, Pepperdine, California Institute for Immunology and Immunotherapy, Center for Quantum Science and Engineering
