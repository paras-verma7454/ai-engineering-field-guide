ABOUT BESTOW

Life insurance is one of the world's most important products. It's also one of the hardest to build, distribute, and modernize. Bestow exists to change that.

Bestow is a leading vertical technology platform serving some of the largest and most innovative life insurers. Our platform unifies the fragmented, legacy value chain, enabling carriers to launch products in weeks instead of years. Carriers choose us to scale and operate at unprecedented speed, powered by AI and automation.

Bestow isn't selling policies. We're building the infrastructure that helps an entire industry move faster, reach more people, and deliver on its promise.

Backed by leading investors (Goldman Sachs, Hedosophia, NEA, Valar, 8VC) and trusted by major carriers, Bestow is powered by a team that moves with precision, purpose, and heart. If you want to help reimagine a centuries-old industry with lasting impact, join us.

Bestow offers flexible remote/hybrid work, meaningful benefits, equity, and substantial growth opportunities.

Bestow uses E-Verify to confirm the employment eligibility of all newly hired employees. To learn more about E-Verify, including your rights and responsibilities, please visit E-Verify.gov.

ABOUT THE TEAM

We are hiring an AI-Native Quality Engineer to help establish the future of our software validation and verification capabilities in the age of AI.

This role is designed for an early-career engineer with strong experience in artificial intelligence, machine learning workflows, or modern automation tooling who wants to apply those skills to one of the hardest unsolved problems in all of software engineering: turning product intent into trustworthy, testable, verifiable system behavior.

You will work at the intersection of application engineering, product development, and applied AI to help translate ideas into structured specifications, executable examples, and scalable validation systems. Along the way, you will learn modern Quality Engineering disciplines such as Definition of Ready, Example Mapping, Specification by Example, and behavior-focused acceptance criteria – while helping us explore how AI can transform those practices.

This role is a discipline-building apprenticeship with meaningful technical ownership from day one and mentorship from senior engineering leaders shaping next-generation specification-driven delivery systems.

WHAT YOU’LL DO

Software teams now build faster than ever–but confidence still depends on how clearly behavior is defined, validated, and verified.

We are investing in a new generation of engineers who can combine:

  • AI-native thinking

  • structured system reasoning

  • specification clarity

  • automation fluency

  • validation and verification disciplines

to help modernize how software organizations measure correctness and readiness.

You will help shape the future here.

Automation & Verification Engineering (~30%)

  • Contribute to API verification using REST testing tools and schema-based checks

  • Help translate structured examples into executable verification artifacts

  • Support UI and integration automation aligned with documented behavior

  • Assist with test data setup and environment readiness

  • Contribute to CI/CD verification workflows

  • Analyze unexpected behavior discovered during automated execution

  • Improve signal quality from automated feedback loops

Specification & Behavioral Clarity (~30%)

  • Support structured refinement activities that make software behavior explicit before implementation begins

  • Participate in Example Mapping and specification workshops with engineers and product managers

  • Translate feature ideas into structured scenarios and acceptance criteria

  • Capture assumptions, edge cases, and behavioral expectations early in development

  • Help create reusable specification templates and example libraries

  • Apply AI-assisted workflows to improve requirements clarity and consistency

  • Identify ambiguity before it becomes rework

AI-Enabled Quality Engineering Exploration (~40%)

  • Prototype AI-assisted approaches to specification generation

  • Experiment with prompt-driven verification workflows

  • Explore semantic regression detection techniques

  • Evaluate machine-interpretable documentation strategies

  • Integrate AI-assisted tooling into requirements and specifications, test generation, maintenance, failure analysis, and reporting workflows

  • Help identify innovative opportunities to improve engineering confidence using modern AI tooling

WHO YOU ARE

  • 0-2 years of experience in software engineering, AI/ML workflows, or applied automation

  • Strong proficiency in Python, JavaScript/TypeScript, or another modern programming language

  • Hands-on experience working with APIs or modern web systems

  • Demonstrated interest in artificial intelligence, prompt engineering, or evaluation workflows

  • Ability to reason about system behavior through structured examples

  • Strong written communication skills

  • Curiosity about how complex systems behave under changing conditions

  • Interest in learning modern validation and verification disciplines

Preferred Experience

  • LLM experimentation or prompt engineering

  • Evaluation harnesses or benchmarking workflows

  • Semantic search or embeddings workflows

  • Agent pipelines or structured output systems

  • Behavior-driven development practices

  • Demonstrated experience with at least one orchestration/agent framework (LangChain, LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen, or similar).

  • Familiarity with RAG architectures, including vector databases (Pinecone, Weaviate, pgvector) and embedding pipelines.

  • Preference for candidates that submit a link to their GitHub Repository

WHY THIS ROLE IS UNIQUE

  • You will help establish the future of our software validation and verification capabilities in the age of AI.

  • You will work on real product behavior – not synthetic exercises.

  • You will collaborate directly with senior engineers to shape next-generation Quality Engineering practices.

  • You will help explore how artificial intelligence changes the way software correctness is defined and measured.

  • You will grow into a rare engineering discipline at the intersection of AI and other disciplines:

    • specifications

    • automation

    • systems behavior

    • verification science

What you need to know about the Los Angeles Tech Scene

Los Angeles is a global leader in entertainment, so it’s no surprise that many of the biggest players in streaming, digital media and game development call the city home. But the city boasts plenty of non-entertainment innovation as well, with tech companies spanning verticals like AI, fintech, e-commerce and biotech. With major universities like Caltech, UCLA, USC and the nearby UC Irvine, the city has a steady supply of top-flight tech and engineering talent — not counting the graduates flocking to Los Angeles from across the world to enjoy its beaches, culture and year-round temperate climate.

Key Facts About Los Angeles Tech

  • Number of Tech Workers: 375,800; 5.5% of overall workforce (2024 CompTIA survey)
  • Major Tech Employers: Snap, Netflix, SpaceX, Disney, Google
  • Key Industries: Artificial intelligence, adtech, media, software, game development
  • Funding Landscape: $11.6 billion in venture capital funding in 2024 (Pitchbook)
  • Notable Investors: Strong Ventures, Fifth Wall, Upfront Ventures, Mucker Capital, Kittyhawk Ventures
  • Research Centers and Universities: California Institute of Technology, UCLA, University of Southern California, UC Irvine, Pepperdine, California Institute for Immunology and Immunotherapy, Center for Quantum Science and Engineering
